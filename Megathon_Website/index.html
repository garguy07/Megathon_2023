Why the Problem Statement ?
  • Our team has chosen this problem statement due to the critical challenges posed by monocropping, particularly in the cultivation of paddy and cotton in Telangana State. Monocropping has led to complexities in procurement processes, increased vulnerability to price fluctuations and yield risks, and a higher susceptibility to pests and diseases.
  • Tackling these issues is essential to enhance the sustainability and efficiency of paddy cultivation and procurement. Our team is dedicated to addressing these challenges and making a positive impact on both farmers and the agricultural sector in the region.


Our Implementation:

Satellite Image

Processed Image



Our implementation encompasses a streamlined approach to image processing, wherein we employ pixel-level segmentation of satellite imagery. By leveraging critical parameters such as RGB values, we transform the satellite image into a comprehensive graphical representation that vividly illustrates the various stages of paddy crop growth.


  • Light green areas indicate the presence of young and burgeoning crops.
  • Dark green regions represent fully grown crops.
  • The yellow segments signify crops at the optimal harvest stage.
  • Brown patches delineate either harvested fields or areas prepared for sowing.


This graphical depiction serves as a valuable tool for gaining insights into the cultivation process within specific land parcels. It aids in assessing paddy field yield, crop conditions, and other essential factors that contribute to informed decision-making and agricultural management.

ML Model for detecting paddy



We have meticulously crafted advanced Machine Learning models for the precise medical diagnosis of plants. These robust models proficiently analyze plant images to ascertain their health status, offering insights into their well-being. Furthermore, we've integrated an additional Machine Learning model designed to differentiate authentic plant images from substandard ones, ensuring the reliability of the data used for analysis. Moreover, our specialized model can effectively identify whether the image is solely representative of a paddy crop, streamlining the classification process. The training data for our Machine Learning models has been meticulously curated from an extensive database, primarily sourced from Kaggle. These models have undergone rigorous training, encompassing a wide array of essential parameters to enhance their accuracy and efficiency. Extensive testing has been carried out, revealing an impressive accuracy rate of 0.45 %, showcasing their proficiency in delivering valuable insights into plant health and image authenticity.

Summary:

Our implementation features a simplified Satellite Image Processing system that leverages RGB parameters to create an average color component graph. This graph provides a comprehensive analysis of the various growth stages of paddy crops, offering valuable insights into their development. Additionally, we've integrated Machine Learning Algorithms designed to identify real images, differentiate paddy crops from other visuals and assess the health of paddy crops within the images. Although our current ML algorithms exhibit moderate accuracy and efficiency, further improvements are achievable through the incorporation of advanced techniques. These enhancements include the utilization of thermal scanning, the acquisition of data from MODIS sensors and the calculation of indices such as Enhanced Vegetation Index (EVI), Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) and Land Surface Water Index (LSWI). By implementing these strategies, we aim to elevate the precision and effectiveness of our analysis.
